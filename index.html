Questions

Good to know

What kind of tasks is Due Again for?

The infrequent, recurring upkeep that's easy to forget — replacing a water filter every few months, servicing the car each year, testing smoke alarms, bleeding the radiators, descaling the kettle. Anything whose interval is long enough that you can't keep it in your head.

What's the difference between fixed and completion-based schedules?

A fixed schedule anchors each occurrence to the calendar — every 12 months from the start date — so the cadence stays put regardless of when you actually do it. A completion-based schedule measures the next period from your last completion, so it shifts to "X months after I last did it." You can also create simple one-off tasks.

Do I need an account to start?

No. You can start tracking tasks right away. You only sign in when you want to share tasks with someone or sync across devices.

How does household sharing work?

The owner of a task sends an invite link. The person you invite can join as a viewer (sees the task and its history) or an editor (can also complete and edit it). When anyone completes a shared task, everyone is notified and all reminders update to the new due date.

What do I get for free, and what does Pro add?

Free covers up to 4 active tasks and sharing with 1 person, with all schedule types, reminders and full history included. Due Again Pro removes those limits — unlimited tasks and unlimited household sharing — for $2.99/month or $19.99/year.

What platforms is Due Again on?

Due Again is available on iPhone, with reminders delivered as native notifications. It's built on a secure backend so sharing and sync work reliably across everyone in your household.
